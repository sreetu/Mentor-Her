Mentoring Girls living in Rural Areas


Most of the rural girls in India are unable to complete ev en their H igh S chool; approximately only 1 out of 50 girls receives any post-secondary education. This lack of opportunity is due to the effects of chronic poverty, cultural beliefs that education is “wasted” on girls, long-distance walks to school, lack of sanitary support, domestic violence, and other challenges.

Although these girls currently live in situations that give them little hope for a future that doesn’t include poverty or marriage at a young age, they have dreams of becoming educated, independent women who can effect change in India in a variety of ways.

Mentor-Her will provide a platform for them to ask questions regarding any topic without any hesitation. This platform will provide a community of open-minded girls and women to support them and raise their standard of livings.
